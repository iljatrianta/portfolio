UNDER
THE
DUST

An interactive multimedia story that brings to life the history of the mining industry in the Limburg province, making it engaging and accessible for everyone.

DURATION
10 weeks

TEAM
4 students

CLIENT
Maastricht University Library - Special Collection

MY ROLE
Research, Conceptual Design, Website development

THE
PROBLEM

The Central Bureau of Statistics struggles with making their data engaging, most people see it as boring or too plain to really pay attention to.

They asked us to explore how their statistics could be presented in a more visually interesting and appealing way.

For this project, we focused on data about the local mining history in the Limburg province, Netherlands (where my school is located), and the city of Heerlen in particular, which was the center of the mining industry in that region. With the goal of turning this information into something people actually want to explore and interact with.

“Turn dry statistics about Limburg’s mining history into something people actually want to see, explore, and understand.”

FIELD RESEARCH

To better understand the topic, we visited mining museums and explored the history of Limburg’s mining industry firsthand.

We also spoke with people who lived during the mining era, gaining personal insights that added depth to the data.

Finally, we engaged with our target audience to understand what captures their attention and what makes them interested in this kind of information.

INSIGHTS

We discovered that people engage more with Limburg’s history when it is told in an interactive and immersive way. Raw data alone did not hold much attention, but stories that connect facts to real people and emotions did.

We also decided to focus on Heerlen, a city in Limburg, because it was the center of the mining era and was deeply shaped by it. The locals we spoke to were very passionate about this period and wanted to share their stories and keep this part of their heritage alive.

We also found that Heerlen still has a bad reputation among outsiders, largely because of its mining history. At the same time, locals want people to understand where that image comes from and why that history still matters today.

THE CONCEPT

We created an interactive multimedia story that lets users scroll through the history of Limburg using text, video, audio, and images. The format is designed to increase immersion and build a stronger emotional connection with the region’s past and its people. We also integrated statistics provided by the client directly into the narrative to ground the story in real data.

The project focuses on the history of Heerlen, a city often associated with a negative reputation. We explore how the mining industry shaped both its development and the public perception it carries today.

Users can dive deeper into specific parts of the story if they want more detail. This keeps the main experience clear and digestible, while still offering depth and source material for those who are interested.

Heerlen went through many changes after the mines closed. The city changed from one of the most prestigious in the country to one of the poorest and most unsafe. To show the different eras and emotions that came with this, we used different colors for each period.

The bright colors also create a strong contrast with the black-and-white archival images from that time period we used throughout the project.

FINAL

We turned our concept into a multimedia story website that we got to share with local schools so children can learn about their city’s history in a more engaging way.

It's important to note that this story was originally written in Dutch, and the version you see here has been translated into English for this portfolio, so there might be some differences in phrasing and missing cultural references that don't translate well.
