GOGO
VISUALS

A set of live background visuals created for Gogo, a techno music artist and producer, to be displayed during his live performance at our school’s music festival.

DURATION
10 weeks

TEAM
only me

CLIENT
Gogo - Techno music artist and producer

MY ROLE
Research, Conceptual Design, Visual Design, Coding

THE
REQUEST

Gogo, a techno music artist and producer, wanted us to create live background visuals for the upcoming live music festival in our school.

He wanted the visuals to match the vibe and story of his music, and the visuals should dynamically respond to his music during the performance.

“Create live background visuals that complements Gogo’s music and create an immersive experience for the audience.”

THE ARTIST

Gogo creates dark, atmospheric techno rooted in mood and memory. A major source of inspiration is his home country, Bulgaria, and the way local culture can slowly fade when it is not preserved. That sense of disappearing heritage shaped the story and sound of his music.

RESEARCH

Since Gogo’s music is inspired by disappearing culture, I started by researching cultures and visual traditions that have faded or disappeared over time.

From there, I explored patterns, shapes, and colors of cultures that have already disappeared completely. After discussing the direction with Gogo, he suggested including a pattern from Bulgarian culture as well, which became part of the final design.

Research, reading, sketching and prototypes...

DESIGN

The concept was to bring back patterns and visual elements from cultures that have disappeared (like the Minoans, the Indus people, the Maya,...), so I made a set of patterns all inspired by ancient cultures that I later used throughout the design.

I made a total of 3 different scenes where all the patterns are arranged and move differently, the layout of each scene takes inspiration from traditional Bulgarian patterns and embroidery art.

FINAL DESIGN

The final result was displayed on a big screen during Gogo's performance at the school's music festival. The visuals were made completely in code and respond to the music in real time. I also created my own control panel with buttons and sliders to control the visuals during the live performance.
